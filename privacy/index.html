Privacy Policy

Effective Date: January 2025

At RLC Advocates LLP, your privacy is important to us. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, protect, and disclose your personal information when you visit our website or engage with our services.

By using our website or services, you consent to the practices described in this policy.

1. What Information We Collect

When you visit our website or contact us, we may collect your:

  • Contact details (e.g., name, email address, phone number) when you fill out forms or contact us.
  • Professional information (e.g., company name, position) for business-related communications.
  • Technical data such as IP address, browser type, and usage patterns through cookies and analytics tools.
  • Any other information you voluntarily provide when communicating with us.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use your information to:

  • Respond to your inquiries and provide legal services.
  • Manage client relationships and billing.
  • Improve our website and services.
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations.
  • Communicate important updates or offers related to our services (only if you have opted in).

3. Third-Party Links

Our website may contain links to third-party websites. We are not responsible for the privacy practices or content of these sites. We encourage you to review their privacy policies before providing personal information.

4. International Data Transfers

If you are located outside our jurisdiction, your information may be transferred to and processed in our country. We ensure appropriate safeguards are in place to protect your data in accordance with Data Protection Act, 2019 and all applicable laws.

5. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, loss, or misuse of the said personal data. We protect your information with security measures and only share it with authorized persons who help us run our business.

We do not sell or rent your personal data to third parties. We may share your information with:

  • Service providers who assist with our business operations (under confidentiality agreements).
  • Legal or regulatory authorities when required by law or to protect our legal rights.

6. Cookies

Our website uses cookies to make your visit better. Cookies are small files saved on your device. You can turn them off in your browser settings, but some parts of the site might not work well.

7. Your Rights

You have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction or deletion of inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Object to or restrict certain processing of your data.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.
  • Lodge a complaint with the relevant data protection authority.

8. How Long We Keep Your Data

We keep your data only as long as we need it to provide our services or follow the law.

9. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Changes will be posted on this page with an updated "Last Updated" date. We encourage you to review this policy periodically.
